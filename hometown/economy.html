Economy


A wide range of different industries including tourism, small companies, and agriculture contribute to Creston’s economy and surrounding communities. Local farms and orchards produce fruits, wine and dairy products that are sold across the region. You’ll see rows of fruit trees, fields of grain, and roadside stands and fruit markets selling fresh produce throughout the summer. Many of the family run farms have been passed down for generations.

Beyond farming, Creston has a large amount of small local businesses that give the town its charm. You’ll find cozy cafes, handmade craft shops, and family owned restaurants that serve food made from local ingredients. The town is also home to a number of craft breweries, distilleries, and artisanal shops that attract visitors year round. Tourism and the service industry provide more jobs and opportunities for small business owners, while visitors come to to the valley. Many residents work in farming, retail, education, healthcare, and trades. In Creston, people tend to support one another. When you buy something from a local shop or visit a farm stand, you’re not just helping your neighbors but your helping keep the town thriving.

Well-known Important Businesses

Kootenay Meadows

Kootenay Meadows is a family run dairy farm in Creston. They sell fresh organic dairy products at many of the shops in town.

J.H. Huscroft Ltd.

The Huscroft sawmill has been part of Creston’s economy for decades, providing steady work for many residents and supporting the valley’s long-standing forestry industry.

Columbia Brewery

The Columbia Brewery is another major employer in Creston, the brewery produces Kokanee beer and plays an important role in supporting the local economy.
