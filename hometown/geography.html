CRESTON

WELCOME TO THE KOOTENAYS

Geography

Creston can be found in the southern region of British Columbia, between the Selkirk and Purcell mountain ranges just thirty minutes from the Idaho border. The town is located on the unceded traditional territory of the Yaqan Nukiy land within the Ktunaxa Nations (Town Profile, n.d.). The landscape is rich and green, filled with gorgeous mountain ranges, farmland, forests, and wetlands that attract a huge variety of wildlife, with the Kootenay and Goat River flowing nearby.


The mild climate and fertile soil make it one of the best agricultural regions in the province for growing fresh fruit, grains, and vegetables. With every season the scenery changes, with blossoms in spring, red and orange leaves crawling up the mountains in fall, and soft snowy mountain peaks in winter. Because the valley is open and surrounded by nature, life here makes you feel more connected to the land.

More Info

Creston is home to an abundance of wildlife including black bears and grizzly bears. Make sure to bring bear spray on hikes and stay safe in forested areas.

Hundreds of elk flood the Creston flats on early mornings. If you are lucky, you can experience a majestic sight on your way to work.

If you love the outdoors Creston is surrounded by many beautiful hiking trails and paths. Ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes.
