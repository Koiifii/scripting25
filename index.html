About Me :)

I am a first-year student attending the Multimedia Production Program at Lethbridge Polytechnic with the intention of turning my passion for creativity into a professional career. Ever since I could remember creating things, and making art has always brought me so much happiness that not a lot of other things did. Other than being able to express myself in ways you can’t really with words, letting my creativity spiral has helped me ground myself. and feel more connected from within. Every year I love taking on new hobbies and growing my abilities. Throughout my life I have taken on a variety of different mediums like painting, drawing, sculpting, felting, woodburning, and drawing digitally on my iPad. I would love to be able to take my skills and turn them into a living where I can create things that not only I would love and be proud of but also the people I would be working with.

Accomplishments


I consider myself to be an incredibly strong and resilient individual. As someone who loved school as a young girl, High school was harder than I thought. I had a rather rough upbringing, but I am so proud of my younger self for facing the challenges that no young teenage girl should have to face. I was able to power through and graduate while really struggling with bullying and mental health. I came out stronger and became the person who I am today, which I would consider one of my greatest accomplishments. When I was younger, I also loved theatre and took part in a handful of community musical productions. In the 6th grade, I played one of the lead roles “Gertrude Mcfuzz”, in Seussical the Muscical that. I had to sing around 6 solos, which I don’t think I could ever do now.

Personal Life

Growing up, I was raised by my mom, who I would consider my best friend. I was born on Vancouver Island then moved to Clearwater, BC. Eventually, decided to settle in Creston. I grew up with my older and younger brothers. For the majority of my life, I have looked up to my older brother Quin, who has been a major creative inspiration.

Animals have always held a special place in my heart. I have helped rehabilitate a family of baby skunks, raising them to adulthood before releasing them back into the wild, and assisted in fostering baby kittens. In September 2023, I adopted a puppy. Though not having set plans for the future yet, I wasn’t sure if it was the best idea. Little did I know that sweet, little miracle pup Gypsy was everything I ever needed. Before moving to Lethbridge for school, I would spend a lot of my free time going on adventures and hikes around the area with my fluffy best friend. My love for family and animals influences the way I approach my work, with care, patience, and a sense of gratitude.
